What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is the capacity to perceive, comprehend, and regulate one's own emotions, as well as to recognize and influence the emotions of others. It involves a suite of competencies that empower individuals to navigate social contexts adeptly, foster and sustain relationships, manage adversities, and make informed decisions grounded in emotional acumen.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Distinguishing itself from Intelligence Quotient (IQ), Emotional Intelligence focuses on emotional literacy, empathetic attunement, interpersonal adeptness, and emotional self-control. Unlike IQ, which predominantly assesses cognitive prowess like logical reasoning and problem-solving abilities, EQ emphasizes understanding and managing the complexities of human emotions and social interactions.

Why is EQ important?

EQ underscores its pivotal role in various facets of life. From fostering robust interpersonal connections and effective leadership to bolstering personal resilience and enhancing decision-making, EQ serves as a linchpin for success and well-being. By honing emotional intelligence, individuals can harness the power of emotions to navigate life's challenges, cultivate meaningful relationships, and lead fulfilling lives characterized by authenticity and empathy.
