Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

One of them is definitely my time management skills. i have never been this busy before. I am working four days a week, studying for a master's degree and studying for this certificate in applied software development. On top of those, I try to keep myself active outside work and study, so I take weekly salsa dance sessions and tennis sessions. I have been busy but I am enjoying all of these. The second one would be that I have progressed a lot. I didn't realise it until I passed a technical assessment from a graduate program I applied for. I am really proud of myself. Third thing is I can't believe how easily distracted I am sometimes. If I want to concertrate on something, I have to leave my phone far away from me. Otherwise I always end up with scrolling on my phone for nothing.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think it is of vital importance, as this is about how to view challenges in the process of programming. I learned it long time ago that a big part of programming is testing and dubugging. No one can guarantee that all the codes they write at the first time work staightaway.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I didn't know the term neuroplasticity, and it is good to develop a better understanding of it.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Nothing really. I learned most of it in my previous work experience.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Because in a real project in the real world, it is collaboration that matters. There will be teamwork where effective listening is needed. Besides, growth mindset is significant as tech itself is developing in a fast pace nowadays, especially with AI. Developers need to get their growth mindset ready, which is why I hope to be a developer as it feels great to always learn new stuff.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't feel that way. Core learning is actually the key to future growth from my perspective, because in the real case there is communication and more needed working in the team.
